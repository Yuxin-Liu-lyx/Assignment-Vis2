Domestic Flights in Australia

This is a dashboard of domestic flights in Australia in June 2019. Designed by Xinyi Wu.








Routes and Passengers of Flights

On the right is a map of Australia. Hover on a departure city and the routes will emerge. The symbol size in this map is based on the number of passengers.

As can be seen from the map, Brisbane has the largest number of passengers and the largest number of domestic routes. This suggests that Brisbane is a popular departure city during this period.








Number of Trips, Loading factor and Passengers

This bubble plot looks closely at the data of domestic flights in June, 2019. Load factor measures the percentage of available seating capacity that has been filled with passengers. A high load factor is preferred over a low load factor.

The departure city with the highest loading factor is Darwin, which is 83.53%. However, it has a relatively small number of trips.




Comparison of Flights from 2018 to 2020

In 2020, the lockdown in Australia took a heavy toll on the domestic airline industry. As can be seen from the chart on the left, the passengers of domestic flights decreased until April.

Similarly, the close of national border took a heavy toll on the number of international flights, which is shown in the chart on the right.
